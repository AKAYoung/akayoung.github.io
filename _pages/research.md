# Research

My research focuses on non-intrusive load monitoring, smart grid applications, and time-series deep learning. I am particularly interested in improving appliance-level load perception under data-scarce, cross-domain, and resource-constrained scenarios.

## Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring

Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring aims to infer appliance-level power consumption from aggregate household or building-level power signals. My work studies NILM as a time-series modeling and source separation problem, with a focus on improving disaggregation accuracy and robustness across different usage scenarios.

## Synthetic Load Data Generation

Sparse appliance activations and limited labeled data are common challenges in NILM. To address this issue, I study synthetic load data generation methods that combine autoregressive Transformer modeling and diffusion-based generation to improve the diversity and utility of training samples.

## Cross-Domain Load Disaggregation

NILM models often suffer from performance degradation when transferred across houses, datasets, or usage environments. My research explores parameter-efficient adaptation mechanisms, especially adapter-based tuning and efficient attention, to improve transfer robustness with reduced fine-tuning cost.

## Edge-Oriented NILM

In practical smart grid and energy internet scenarios, NILM models may need to operate under resource-constrained edge conditions. I am interested in lightweight model adaptation, communication-efficient deployment, and edge-cloud collaborative NILM frameworks.
